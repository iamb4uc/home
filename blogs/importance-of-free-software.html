Importance free software for privacy and security

Created and posted by b4uc on Jun 07th 2022


When I talk about free software in public, people usually normies think that I am talking about free as in price, tho I do agree that some basic pieces of software such as system software, application software and utility software do need to be free, I also refer the term FREE as in FREEDOM, freedom here mean the liberty to change, modify and audit the code and the structure of a piece of software. This don't particularly mean that the software should never make profits for its company or creator, this just puts privacy and security of the end user before money.

A creator can make free software and still earn money from it. You can find more about this topic here

With popular free software, you have many people actively auditing, editing and keeping an active eye on the development, which makes vulnerabilities or exploits to pass on to a release.

Ok enough talking shit, lets get down to the main point of writing this blog.

Propiratory software donot disclose about what they collect from you they sometimes can really obtain some granular details like you geolocation, address, or even your most visited locations like your workplace. While this are *mostly* used for targeted ads, but you cannot be certain if they use it for anything other than that, usually propiratory software can be used as modern day wiretaps used by governments to spy on its public and Edward Snowden proved that this practice actually exits and isnt a hoax or conspiracy.

When you use free and open-source software, you have all the freedom of checking for backdoors, audit all of its code and even if you arn't tech-savy you can still have the assureance that a large group of people already are actively checking for vulnerabilities and bugs and other sensitive code that can be exploited.
