    Programming books

    Name: The C Programming Language, 2nd Edition
    Author: Brian W. Kernighan | Dennis M. Ritchie
    Version: 2nd Edition
    ISBN-13: 978-0131103627
    ISBN-10: 0131103628
    Buy: Amazon | Better World Books(used and recommended)

    This book is one of those books that I cant recommend enough, it got me properly started with Standard C or as this book and everyone else descibes it, ANSI C. Tho you would need a little bit of knowledge about programming like knowing what keywords, variables, loops and functions, its worth reading if you want to learn C or interested in an new language



    UNIX and GNU/Linux

    Name: The Linux Command Line, 2nd Edition: A Complete Introduction
    Author: William Shotts
    Version: 2nd Edition
    ISBN-13: 978-1593279523
    ISBN-10: 1593279523
    Buy: no starch press | Amazon

    It is one of those mainstream books that everyone recommends people to read. People who know me know that I dispise the mainstream for a reason idk why? but I do, but this book is different, it is one of those books I tell new people who are getting into GNU/Linux to read, its like an essential books that kinda is a cheatsheat for the terminal, it shows you all the major command you can use to execute tasks on the terminal like manipulating files, creating files etc.


    Name: Wicked Cool Shell Scripts, 2nd Edition: 101 Scripts for Linux, OS X, and UNIX Systems
    Author: Dave Taylor | Brandon Perry
    Version: 2nd Edition
    ISBN-13: 978-1593276027
    ISBN-10: 1593276028
    Buy: no starch press | Amazon

    I cannot recommend this book enough, even if you are new to shell scipting, you can still learn a lot from this book. It can be difficult for you if you never worked with scripts or the command line on a POSIX-complaint operating system but you can surely learn something new.


    Name: Absolute FreeBSD, 3rd Edition: The Complete Guide to FreeBSD
    Author: Michael W. Lucas
    Version: 3rd Edition
    ISBN-13: 978-1593278922
    ISBN-10: 1593278926
    Buy: no starch press | Amazon

    This is the absolutly one of the best books I found on FreeBSD, I really love the way this books covers FreeBSD and dives deep into server management and understanding the workings of the operating system responsible for running some of worlds biggest servers like Netflix, EMC etc.


    Name: TLS Mastery: Tux and Beastie editions
    Author: Michael W. Lucas
    Version: -
    ISBN-13: 978-1642350531
    ISBN-10: 1642350532
    Buy(Tux Edition): Michael W. Lucas(recommended) | Amazon
    Buy(Beastie Edition): Michael W. Lucas(recommended) | Amazon

    This book is a part of 2 books ie TLS Mastery by Michael W. Lucas, this make you understand how TLS works and what it actually is. In this day and age, TLS is the most basic need we require in a website, it provides a secured connection ie encryption for most of the major ecommerce and banking sites along with other sites that lets you have a unique profile and lets you log into that profile. It protects your passwords and your privacy. Let's Encrypt transformed TLS from an expensive tool to a free one. TLS understanding and debugging is an essential sysadmin skill you must have.
