    Operating Systems

    Topics: BSD, Linux, UNIX

    Name: The Linux Command Line, 2nd Edition: A Complete Introduction
    Author: William Shotts
    Publisher:
    Version: 2nd Edition
    ISBN-13: 978-1593279523
    ISBN-10: 1593279523
    Buy: Thriftbooks(used) | No Starch Press | Amazon

    It is one of those mainstream books that everyone recommends people to read. People who know me know that I dispise the mainstream for a reason idk why? but I do, but this book is different, it is one of those books I tell new people who are getting into GNU/Linux to read, its like an essential books that kinda is a cheatsheat for the terminal, it shows you all the major command you can use to execute tasks on the terminal like manipulating files, creating files etc.


    Name: Absolute FreeBSD, 3rd Edition: The Complete Guide to FreeBSD
    Author: Michael W. Lucas
    Publisher:
    Version: 3rd Edition
    ISBN-13: 978-1593278922

    Buy: No Starch Press | Amazon

    This is the absolutly one of the best books I found on FreeBSD, I really love the way this books covers FreeBSD and dives deep into server management and understanding the workings of the operating system responsible for running some of worlds biggest servers like Netflix, EMC etc.


    Name: TLS Mastery: Tux and Beastie editions
    Author: Michael W. Lucas
    Publisher:
    Version: -
    ISBN-13: 978-1642350531
    ISBN-10: 1642350532
    Buy(Tux Edition): Thriftbooks(used) | Michael W. Lucas(recommended) | Amazon
    Buy(Beastie Edition): Michael W. Lucas(recommended) | Amazon

    This books are a part of 2 books ie TLS Mastery by Michael W. Lucas, this make you understand how TLS works and what it actually is. In this day and age, TLS is the most basic thing we need in a website, it provides a secured connection ie encryption for most of the major ecommerce and banking sites along with other sites that lets you have a unique profile and lets you log into that profile. It protects your passwords and your privacy. Let's Encrypt transformed TLS from an expensive tool to a free one. TLS understanding and debugging is an essential sysadmin skill you must have.
